2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account information (only if you create an account / sign in): your email address, and—if you use Google or Apple sign‑in—the basic profile details those services return (such as name and email). Authentication may use one‑time email codes (OTP).
  • Business and invoicing content you enter: your business profile, logo, address and tax identifiers (for example GST/VAT numbers), bank/payment details, and the customers, items, invoices, estimates, expenses, time entries, and payments you record.

  • Support communications: information you provide when you contact us.

2.2 Purchases and subscriptions

In‑app purchases (Invora Pro) are processed by Apple App Store or Google Play and managed through RevenueCat. We do not receive or store your full payment card details. We (via RevenueCat) receive your subscription/entitlement status, a purchase/transaction identifier, the product purchased, and an app user identifier so we can unlock Pro features on your account and devices.

2.3 Information collected automatically

Invora does not include third‑party advertising or analytics SDKs. Limited technical information may be processed by our infrastructure providers to operate the service—for example, server logs (such as IP address and request metadata) generated when the App communicates with our cloud backend, and device information necessary to deliver purchases and push notifications.

3. How your data is stored (local vs. cloud)

  • On your device (default): Invora stores your business data in a local database on your device. If you never sign in, your invoicing data is not transmitted to our servers.
  • In the cloud (optional): If you sign in and enable cloud features (sync, backup, or sharing), your data and document files are stored with our backend provider, Supabase, so they are available across your devices and can be restored.

4. Sharing invoices and public links

If you generate a shareable link for an invoice or estimate, the corresponding document/PDF is uploaded to secure cloud storage and made accessible through a unique, hard‑to‑guess link token so the recipient can view it. The App may record view/“read receipt” information (such as the time a shared document was opened) to show you delivery status. Do not share links containing sensitive information with people who should not see them; anyone with a valid link can view that document.

5. How we use information

  • To provide and operate the App and its core invoicing features.
  • To authenticate you and, if enabled, sync and back up your data across devices.
  • To process and restore in‑app purchases and unlock Pro features.
  • To generate, deliver, and track shared documents you choose to share.
  • To provide customer support and respond to your requests.
  • To maintain security, prevent abuse, and comply with legal obligations.

  • By request: You may also email [CONTACT EMAIL] to request deletion. Subscriptions are managed by the app stores; cancel renewals in your Google Play or Apple account settings.
